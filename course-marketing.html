Week 1: The Business of Sport - Where the Money Flows
‚ñº
Executive: Bell Media / TSN Senior Leader
Case: NHL Vancouver Canucks (HBS W14149)
Time: 5-6 hours

Professional sports teams face a unique business challenge: How do you maximize revenue when your competitors are also your business partners? Week 1 tackles real revenue strategy problems facing leagues and teams.

Learning Outcomes:

  • Map the 5 major revenue streams (media, ticketing, sponsorship, merchandising, betting)
  • Understand sport's unique business model characteristics (revenue sharing, competitive balance)
  • Evaluate growth potential and risk across different revenue sources
  • Analyze revenue sharing models and their strategic implications

Key Scenarios: Revenue Mix Slider, Dynamic Pricing Simulator, Ecosystem Cascade Simulator

Assessment: 1-page reflection memo on most at-risk revenue stream

Week 2: Media, Content & Fan Engagement
‚ñº
Executive: TSN or DAZN Canada Leader
Case: Rogers NHL $5.2B Deal (HBS W16179)
Time: 7-9 hours

Sports content is the last "appointment viewing" in the streaming era. Week 2 explores how media rights are valued, how platform economics differ, and how the Rogers/NHL mega-deal reshaped Canadian sports broadcasting.

Learning Outcomes:

  • Explore how media rights deals are valued using 5-factor framework
  • Compare linear broadcasting economics to streaming/DTC models
  • Understand fan engagement monetization through ads, subscriptions, data
  • Evaluate the Rogers $5.2B NHL deal and analyze what went wrong

Key Scenarios: Streaming Wars Strategy, Media Rights Calculator, Platform Economics Visualizer

Assessment: 2-page strategic memo + Anchor Project Milestone 1 (team formation)

Week 3: Sponsorship & Betting - The Growth Engines
‚ñº
Executives: Andrew Sneyd (FanDuel) + Brand Sponsor
Case: FanDuel: Betting on Sports (HBS 520038)
Time: 4-5 hours

Sponsorship activation and sports betting are the fastest-growing revenue streams in professional sport. Week 3 teaches you how to calculate ROI, design activations, and integrate betting partnerships.

Learning Outcomes:

  • Understand sponsorship as the financial backbone of sport (30-40% of revenue)
  • Analyze sports betting as a new growth driver in the industry
  • Calculate how brands measure ROI in sport partnerships
  • Design sponsorship activations that deliver measurable value

Key Scenarios: Sponsorship ROI Calculator, Betting Market Simulator, Activation Budget Builder

Assessment: Group sprint activation plan (40 pts) + Milestone 2 (revenue model)

Week 4: Athletes, Brands & Emerging Sports
‚ñº
Executives: Matt Rubinoff (Fairgrounds), Niki da Silva & Tim McLaughlin (Golf Canada)
Case: Serena Williams: Brand Strategy Analysis (course-developed)
Time: 6-7 hours

How do athletes build generational wealth? What makes women's sports and emerging leagues investable? Week 4 explores athlete branding strategy and new market opportunities.

Learning Outcomes:

  • Design athlete branding and endorsement strategies for career longevity
  • Analyze women's sport as a growing sponsorship opportunity
  • Evaluate emerging sports (Pickleball, Padel, esports) as new markets
  • Assess investment opportunities in new vs traditional sports properties

Key Scenarios: Athlete Brand Calculator, Emerging Sports Investment Tool, Women's Sports ROI Model

Assessment: Strategic brand memo (100 pts + 5 PAIRR) + Milestone 3 (brand strategy)

Key Scenarios: Legacy vs Innovation Matrix, Heritage Property Analyzer, Future Trends Predictor
