The Business of Sports Marketing

5-Week Graduate Course | QM & UDL Compliant Design

Course Learning Outcomes (CLO)

CLO 1: Analyze Revenue Ecosystems

Map and evaluate the interdependencies of major revenue streams (media rights, sponsorship, ticketing, merchandising, betting, licensing) in sports business

CLO 2: Evaluate Media & Fan Monetization

Compare traditional broadcasting vs DTC models and design fan engagement strategies that maximize revenue

CLO 3: Design Sponsorship & Betting Strategies

Calculate sponsorship ROI and assess sports betting's role as a growth engine in modern sports marketing

CLO 4: Build Athlete & Emerging Sport Brands

Create branding strategies for athletes and evaluate investment opportunities in emerging sports (women's sports, Pickleball, Padel, esports)

CLO 5: Balance Legacy & Innovation

Develop strategies to monetize heritage properties while integrating future trends (tech, betting, new sports)

Anchor Project: Final Project Thread

The Anchor Project is a collaborative, cumulative assessment spanning Weeks 2-5 where student teams develop a comprehensive 5-year strategic vision for a Canadian sports property. This project scaffolds incrementally each week, building from team formation to final strategic vision.

Week 2: Milestone 1 - Team Formation & Property Selection (10 points)

Deliverable: 1-page project proposal

Components:

  • Form teams of 5-6 students using Team Charter Builder tool
  • Select sport property from 19 curated options using Property Selection Tool:
    • Legacy Properties: Toronto FC, Vancouver Whitecaps, Rugby Canada
    • Emerging Sports: Canadian Premier League, National Lacrosse League
    • Major Events: 2026 FIFA World Cup (Toronto/Vancouver)
  • Write 250-word rationale: Why this property? What marketing challenges?

Tools Provided:

  • Property Selection Tool: Interactive widget with 19 property cards, filtering by category/market/revenue tier, comparison view, PDF export
  • Team Charter Builder: Guided process for roles, communication norms, conflict resolution, accountability mechanisms

Skills Developed: Team formation, strategic property selection, defensible business rationale

Week 3: Milestone 2 - Revenue Model & Sponsorship Strategy (Weight TBD)

Deliverable: Revenue model canvas and sponsorship strategy for selected property

Focus Areas:

  • Apply Week 1 revenue ecosystem frameworks to selected property
  • Map property's current revenue mix (media, ticketing, sponsorship, merch, betting)
  • Use Week 3 sponsorship ROI frameworks to identify growth opportunities
  • Develop sponsorship activation concepts with projected returns

Tools Provided:

  • Revenue Model Canvas Builder: Interactive tool for mapping revenue streams
  • Sponsorship ROI Calculator: Financial modeling for partnership value

Connection to Final Vision: This milestone establishes the financial foundation (revenue streams and sponsorship strategy represent 35 points of the final 100-point rubric)

Week 4: Milestone 3 - Brand Strategy & Competitive Analysis (Weight TBD)

Deliverable: Athlete/brand strategy and emerging market analysis for selected property

Focus Areas:

  • Apply Week 4 athlete branding frameworks to property's star athletes or brand ambassadors
  • Analyze competitive positioning in Canadian sports market
  • Evaluate opportunities in emerging sports or women's sports market
  • Develop brand growth strategies with financial projections

Skills Developed: Brand architecture, competitive analysis, emerging market assessment, investment evaluation

Connection to Final Vision: Brand strategy represents 15 points of the final 100-point rubric (CLO 4)

Week 5: Milestone 4 - Strategic Vision Memo (105 points total)

Deliverable: Comprehensive 5-year strategic vision integrating all course frameworks

Assignment: Create a strategic vision for your selected sport property as Chief Strategy Officer presenting to the Board of Directors
Length: 1,500-2,000 words OR 8-10 minute presentation
Due: Week 5, Day 10 (with PAIRR feedback cycle Days 5-7)

Format Options (Choose ONE):

  • Written Memo: Executive strategy document (1,500-2,000 words)
  • Live Presentation: Board pitch with slide deck (8-10 minutes)
  • Video Presentation: Recorded strategic pitch (8-10 minutes)
  • Digital Campaign: Interactive microsite or multimedia deck

Required Sections (All Formats):

  1. Executive Summary (150 words) - Strategic vision at-a-glance
  2. Current State Analysis - Revenue ecosystem mapping (Week 1 frameworks)
  3. Strategic Vision - Integration of Weeks 2-5 frameworks:
    • Media rights strategy (Week 2)
    • Sponsorship and betting integration (Week 3)
    • Brand and athlete partnerships (Week 4)
    • Heritage vs. innovation balance (Week 5)
  4. 5-Year Roadmap - Milestones, investments, success metrics
  5. Risk Analysis & Mitigation - Strategic trade-offs and contingencies

Rubric Breakdown (100 + 5 bonus points):

CLO 1 - Revenue Streams (Week 1): 20 points

CLO 2 - Media Strategy (Week 2): 15 points

CLO 3 - Sponsorship & Betting (Week 3): 15 points

CLO 4 - Athlete/Brand Strategy (Week 4): 15 points

CLO 5 - Legacy & Innovation (Week 5): 20 points

Strategic Synthesis: 15 points (integration quality, trade-off analysis, executive communication)

PAIRR Bonus: +5 points (optional peer + AI feedback engagement)

PAIRR Methodology (Optional +5 Bonus Points):

  1. Phase 1 - Draft Submission (Day 5): Submit 80% complete version for feedback
  2. Phase 2 - Dual Feedback (Day 7):
    • Peer review: Review assigned classmate's draft using rubric (+2 pts)
    • AI feedback: Generate ChatGPT feedback on own draft (+1 pt)
  3. Phase 3 - Comparative Reflection (Day 7): Compare peer vs. AI feedback usefulness (300-400 words, +1 pt)
  4. Phase 4 - Revision (Day 10): Submit final version incorporating feedback (100-point rubric grading)
  5. Phase 5 - Post-Revision Reflection (Day 10): Reflect on revision choices and feedback integration (200-300 words, +1 pt)

Integration Across All Weeks:

  • Week 1: Revenue streams become "monetization levers" with specific projections
  • Week 2: Media rights (typically 40-60% of revenue) with platform strategy and valuation methodology
  • Week 3: Sponsorship activation plans with ROI calculations and betting partnerships
  • Week 4: Athlete brand management, emerging sports opportunities, women's sports investment
  • Week 5: Heritage preservation vs. innovation, future trends (AI, VR/AR, demographics), 5-10 year forecasting

Support Tools Available:

  • Strategy Planning Assistant: AI chat widget for conceptual guidance

Key Design Features:

  • Incremental scaffolding prevents "big project panic"
  • Each week builds specific expertise that feeds the capstone
  • PAIRR methodology develops AI literacy alongside domain expertise
  • Authentic assessment using real Canadian sport properties
  • Multiple format options honor diverse communication strengths

Universal Design for Learning (UDL) Implementation

Multiple Means of Representation

  • Text: HBS cases, industry reports, academic articles
  • Visual: Videos (all captioned), infographics, frameworks, charts
  • Interactive: Widgets and simulations for hands-on learning
  • Video: Executive video content with real-world industry perspectives

Multiple Means of Engagement

  • Individual: Pre-session prep exercises and case analysis
  • Small Group: Collaborative sprints, simulations, debates
  • Large Group: Executive panels, Q&A sessions, case discussions
  • Self-Paced: Interactive widgets and optional extension materials

Multiple Means of Action/Expression

  • Written: Memos, case analyses, strategic plans
  • Verbal: Presentations, debates, executive Q&A participation
  • Analytical: Financial models, ROI calculations, market analysis
  • Creative: Campaign concepts, activation plans, brand strategies
  • Final Project: Four format options to match student strengths

Quality Matters (QM) Alignment

Standard 2.1 - Measurable Learning Outcomes: All CLOs and MLOs use single, measurable action verbs (analyze, evaluate, design, calculate, develop)

Standard 2.2 - Module/Course Alignment: Each week explicitly maps MLOs to CLOs with clear alignment indicators

Standard 3.1 - Content Alignment: Supporting content directly supports each learning outcome with specific readings, videos, and activities

Standard 4.1 - Assessment Alignment: Each assessment measures specific learning outcomes with clear rubrics and criteria

Standard 8.1 - Accessibility: WCAG 2.1 AA compliance, captions, transcripts, keyboard navigation, screen reader support
