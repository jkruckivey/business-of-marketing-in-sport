# Building an Empire: An Athlete Brand Graphic Novel Script
**Week 4: Athletes, Brands & Emerging Sports**
**Style:** Retro Sports Illustrated - 1970s-80s painted sports magazine look with warm colors and visible brushstrokes
**Purpose:** Introduce athlete brand building, personal brand equity, women's sports economics, and legacy beyond competition

---

[COVER PAGE]
Title: BUILDING AN EMPIRE
Visual Description: Painted illustration of a powerful Black woman tennis player (MAYA JORDAN) mid-serve, frozen in athletic perfection. Around her float symbols of her empire: a fashion logo, a beauty product, a venture capital document, a magazine cover with her face. She wears not just tennis whites but also carries the weight of a businesswoman. Behind her, a stadium full of fans morphs into a boardroom full of executives. Gold and purple colors dominate. 16:9 aspect ratio.

Visual Description: Maya at 20, holding up a Grand Slam trophy. Cameras flash everywhere. Nike logos visible on her outfit. The scoreboard shows "M. JORDAN - CHAMPION."

Dialogue (ANNOUNCER - text): "At just 20 years old, Maya Jordan has won her first Grand Slam!"

[PANEL 3]
Shot Type & Angle: Medium shot of negotiation table.
Visual Description: Maya at 24, now with an agent, JAMES CHEN (Asian-American, 40s, sharp suit), across from Nike executives. The contract on the table reads "$20 MILLION / 5 YEARS." Maya looks confident, arms crossed.

Dialogue (JAMES): "My client has won three majors. The $50K deal was a bet. This is what winning costs."

[PANEL 4]
Shot Type & Angle: Close-up on Maya's determined face.
Visual Description: Maya's face, late 20s now, looking at a vision board in her hotel room. The board shows: "FASHION LINE" "BEAUTY BRAND" "VENTURE CAPITAL" "PRODUCTION COMPANY" with magazine clippings and notes.
Caption: PHASE 4 - THE VISION. Maya saw what others didn't.
Dialogue (MAYA - thought): "Tennis made me famous. But famous is just a starting point."

[PAGE 5]
Layout Note: 2 large panels showing the women's sports moment.
Style Note: Historic, triumphant, movement-building energy.

[PANEL 1]
Shot Type & Angle: Wide shot of equal pay press conference.
Visual Description: Maya stands at a podium with other female athletes from different sports. Signs behind them read "EQUAL PAY FOR EQUAL PLAY." Cameras and reporters pack the room. The energy is electric, defiant.
Caption: Maya used her platform for more than profit.
Dialogue (MAYA): "When I started, women's prize money was 50% of men's. We changed that. But sponsorship? We're still fighting."
Dialogue (MAYA): "A male athlete with half my wins makes twice my endorsements. That's not a pipeline problem. That's a perception problem."

[PANEL 2]
Shot Type & Angle: Medium shot of Maya meeting with young female athletes.
Visual Description: Maya sits in a circle with a diverse group of young female athletes—basketball, soccer, gymnastics, tennis. They're in a training facility, informal, mentoring in session.
Caption: She mentored the next generation. Not just on performance. On business.
Dialogue (YOUNG ATHLETE): "How do I get sponsors to take me seriously?"
Dialogue (MAYA): "Stop asking for sponsorship. Offer partnership. Show them your audience. Show them your value. Make them need you."

Layout Note: 3 panels showing the full ecosystem.
Style Note: Triumphant, showing the complete brand architecture.

[PANEL 1]
Shot Type & Angle: Wide shot of Maya's office with view of Manhattan.
Visual Description: Maya in her corner office, Manhattan skyline behind her. She reviews documents with her team—her agent James, a CFO, a marketing director. Multiple screens show different aspects of her empire.
Caption: The athlete became the brand. The brand became the empire.
Dialogue (CFO): "Portfolio valuation is at $500 million. The beauty line alone is worth $150 million."

[PANEL 2]
Shot Type & Angle: Close-up on brand architecture diagram.
Visual Description: A whiteboard showing "MAYA JORDAN BRAND ARCHITECTURE" with five pillars:
- "SPORTS LEGACY" (tennis achievements, endorsements)
- "FASHION EMPIRE" (clothing line, retail)
- "BEAUTY BRAND" (cosmetics, skincare)
- "MEDIA PRODUCTION" (TV, film, content)
- "VENTURE CAPITAL" (investments, startups)
Each pillar has revenue numbers and growth arrows.
Caption: Five brand pillars. ONLY five ways to build athlete equity beyond the game.

[PANEL 3]
Shot Type & Angle: Medium shot of Maya looking at the diagram.
Visual Description: Maya stands before the whiteboard, pointing at the architecture. Her team takes notes. She's teaching, not just running.
Dialogue (MAYA): "Athletic performance fades. Endorsement deals end. But a brand... a brand can outlive any career."
Dialogue (MAYA): "That's what I want you to understand. Build the empire while you have the spotlight."
