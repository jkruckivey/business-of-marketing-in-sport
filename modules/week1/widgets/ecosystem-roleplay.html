Final Results

Short-Term Thinking

D
$156M Final Revenue

What Happened

You squeezed $18M from the playoff run, but burned goodwill. Season ticket renewals dropped to 71%. Without ecosystem foundations or fan loyalty, you're left chasing volatile betting revenue.

The Lesson

Betting is a "receiver," not a driver. It benefits from fan engagement but doesn't create it. Without investing in the ecosystem (ticketing, media), you had no foundation to amplify.

Final Results

Too Little, Too Late

C
$162M Final Revenue

What Happened

You tried to pivot to long-term thinking, but years of chasing growth without building fan engagement left you without the loyalty base to convert. New sponsors saw through the playoff spike.

The Lesson

Ecosystem effects compound over time. Starting with the foundation (ticketing, media) creates cascades that accelerate growth. Starting with "growth" streams means building on sand.

Final Results

The Betting Ceiling

C
$174M Final Revenue

What Happened

Playoff betting promotions worked, adding $22M. But regulatory scrutiny increased, and you hit the ceiling of what betting can deliver without a loyal fanbase driving it.

The Lesson

High-growth streams have hidden constraints. Betting's 35% growth rate is real, but it doesn't compound the same way ecosystem investments do. It's a receiver, not a driver.

Final Results

Partial Recovery

B-
$181M Final Revenue

What Happened

Converting playoff bettors to season ticket holders partially worked. You're building the ecosystem now—but you lost three years where those cascade effects could have been compounding.

The Lesson

Sequence matters. Build the ecosystem first (ticketing, media), then layer on growth streams (betting, merch). The cascade effects multiply everything that comes after.
