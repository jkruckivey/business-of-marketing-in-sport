Understanding the Prompt

First, I always read the prompt twice and identify the key requirements. Then I check the rubric to understand how I'll be evaluated.

Key Requirements Identified
  • One-page memo advising a private equity firm
  • Explain unique characteristics of sport's revenue model
  • Provide 2-3 investment decision factors
My Outline Planning Stage

Quick Outline:

  • Opening: Set up context (1-2 sentences)
  • Section 1: How sports revenue is unique (150-200 words)
  • Section 2: 2-3 investment decision factors (150-200 words)
  • Closing: Bottom-line recommendation (1-2 sentences)

This takes 5 minutes and saves 30 minutes of rewriting.

Rubric Check Revenue Stream Analysis (10 pts) and Investment Factors (10 pts) are equally weighted. Don't spend 80% of your memo on one.

Writing the Header

The memo header is standard business format. Details matter—they signal professionalism before anyone reads your analysis.

Executive Memo Word Count: 24
TO: Brookfield Capital Investment Committee
FROM: [Your Name], Sports Business Consultant
DATE: [Current Date]
RE: Professional Sports Team Investment Analysis
Header Best Practices
  • Use full recipient name, not just "Investment Committee"
  • Include your title—it establishes credibility
  • Make the subject line specific and descriptive

Writing the Analysis

The opening hooks the reader, then I deliver on my promise with specific claims supported by evidence and case references.

Executive Memo Word Count: 156
TO: Brookfield Capital Investment Committee
FROM: [Your Name], Sports Business Consultant
DATE: [Current Date]
RE: Professional Sports Team Investment Analysis

Professional sports teams operate under a fundamentally different economic model than traditional businesses. Before committing $500M-$1B, you must understand three characteristics that create both opportunity and risk.

1. Revenue Ecosystem Interdependence

Media rights (55% of NHL revenue) depend on competitive balance maintained through revenue sharing. The Nashville Predators case demonstrates this: despite market challenges, the team must evaluate how different markets affect their revenue mix.

The Pattern: Claim + Evidence + Application Every assertion needs support. Notice how I cite specific percentages from course content and reference the case study.

Investment Factors

For the investment factors, I think like Sarah Chen—what would a PE investor actually care about? Downside protection and upside potential.

Executive Memo Word Count: 312

Investment Decision Factors

Two factors should guide your due diligence:

1. Revenue sharing structure: Prioritize leagues/teams where sharing protects downside. Calculate the "floor" revenue a team receives regardless of performance.

2. Emerging stream exposure: Evaluate the team's position in betting partnerships and digital content rights. These streams (projected 15-20% of revenue by 2030) sit outside traditional sharing pools, creating upside for first-movers.

Be Specific and Actionable Not just "look at revenue sharing"—tell them exactly WHAT to calculate. Not "consider growth potential"—cite specific projections.

Revision and Polish

I have a draft. Now I check if everything earns its place, cut redundancy, and add a clear recommendation.

Take a Position Executives hire consultants to make recommendations, not hedge. "I recommend proceeding to Phase 2" is a clear verdict.

Key Takeaways

  1. Read the prompt and rubric before writing
  2. Make an outline to organize your thinking
  3. Support every claim with specific evidence
  4. Revise for concision—every word earns its place
  5. Check against the rubric before submitting

Give yourself 60-90 minutes for your first draft. You've got this!
