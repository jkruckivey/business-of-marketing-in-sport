The 4 Value Drivers

1
Audience Size
More viewers = more ad impressions = higher value. NFL averages 17M viewers; NHL national broadcasts typically draw 0.5-1.2M.
NFL 17M avg
NHL 0.5-1.2M
2
Demographics
Advertisers pay premium for the 18-49 demographic. Sports audiences skew younger and more affluent than most scripted TV.
Key demo 18-49
Income Above avg
3
Exclusivity
Live sports lose value when watched later, so audiences watch in real time. Viewers see ads because they won't skip ahead.
Sports Mostly live
Scripted Often delayed
4
Scarcity
The NFL concentrates massive audiences into a small number of nationally televised games, creating scarcity that drives bidding wars.
NFL nat'l/wk 3-5
NHL nat'l/wk 2-4

Key Insight

Sports are "appointment viewing" — fans watch live because outcomes matter in real-time. This makes sports one of the last reliable sources of mass, engaged audiences in a fragmented media landscape. The average NFL game attracts more viewers than the World Series, NBA Finals, or Oscars.

Media rights value is driven not just by audience size, but by predictability and advertiser confidence. That's why broadcasters pay billions: not just for viewers, but for viewers who actually watch the ads.
