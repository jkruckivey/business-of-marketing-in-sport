The 5 Revenue Streams

How professional sports teams make money

Media Rights — 40%
Ticketing — 25%
Sponsorship — 20%
Merchandise — 10%
Betting/Gaming — 5%
Media Rights $40M
Growth: 8%/yr
Stability: High

TV and streaming broadcast rights. Long-term contracts (5-10 years) provide predictable revenue but limit flexibility.

Ticketing $25M
Growth: 5%/yr
Stability: High

Season tickets, single-game sales, premium seating. Capacity-constrained but creates direct fan relationships.

Sponsorship $20M
Growth: 12%/yr
Stability: Medium

Corporate partnerships, naming rights, jersey sponsors. Value tied to team visibility and fan engagement.

Merchandise $10M
Growth: 10%/yr
Stability: Variable

Jerseys, apparel, memorabilia. Highly dependent on team performance and star players.

Betting/Gaming $5M
Growth: 35%/yr
Stability: Low

Sports betting partnerships, fantasy sports. Fastest-growing but faces regulatory uncertainty.

Key Observation

Notice that the highest-growth stream (betting at 35%) is the smallest revenue source, while the largest stream (media rights) grows more slowly. Growth rate alone doesn't tell the whole story.
