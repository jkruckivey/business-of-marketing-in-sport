Understanding the Projection Failures

Rogers' projections missed by an average of -$75M per year due to four critical misjudgments:

  • Cord-Cutting Acceleration: Cable/satellite subscribers declined 8-12% annually vs. projected 2-3%. Linear TV advertising revenue collapsed faster than Rogers anticipated.
  • Streaming Underperformance: Sportsnet NOW peaked at 450K subscribers vs. 1M target (55% miss). Streaming revenue couldn't offset linear TV losses.
  • NHL Viewership Decline: Average viewership fell 19% (1.6M → 1.3M) due to weak Canadian team performance, younger demographics abandoning live TV, and competition from highlight clips/social media.
  • COVID-19 Black Swan: Years 6-7 (2019-21) saw revenue collapse with no force majeure clause. Rogers paid full $433M/year despite no fans, bubble playoffs, and 23% ratings decline.
Key Insight: Rogers' single-property bet (NHL-only) created concentration risk. When NHL underperformed, there were no other sports to offset losses. Competitors like TSN with multi-sport portfolios (CFL, NBA, FIFA, MLS) achieved profitability by spreading risk.
