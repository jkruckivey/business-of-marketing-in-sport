Exclusivity Terms - Market Impact

The exclusivity clause gave Rogers a near-monopoly on NHL content in Canada, reshaping the competitive landscape:

  • TSN Lost National Rights: Previously co-held national games with CBC since 2004, completely shut out after 2014
  • CBC Sublicensed from Rogers: CBC retained "Hockey Night in Canada" brand name but paid Rogers for free-to-air Saturday broadcasts ‚Äî no longer an independent rights holder
  • National Exclusivity: No other Canadian broadcaster could acquire NHL games for national distribution (2014-2026 term)
  • Limited Regional Exceptions: Some teams retained existing regional TV deals (e.g., TSN kept Ottawa Senators regional rights, but Rogers still controlled national appearances)
Result: Rogers controlled 95%+ of NHL content distribution in Canada. This monopoly position allowed aggressive pricing but created enormous risk if viewership declined.
